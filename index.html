Hello and Welcome!!!

My name is Strayer Russell. I'm a programmer, electronics/computer hobbyist, general tinkerer, and lover of most things that blink, beep, or have some manner of lightning rock driving their primary functions.

I am currently attending The University of Texas at Dallas where I am majoring in Arts, Technology, and Emerging Communications (A.T.E.C.) with a focus on general design and user experience.

I also work at the Media Archeology Lab, where my coworkers and I collect, restore, and maintain older-ish technology to provide hands-on interactive experiences to other members of the student body. Our exhibits range from exhibitions of Windows-95 and Mac-SE's to R.E.D. our award winning showdog (VCF-SW 2025 Best Restoration), originally built by XEROX PARC in 1999.

This website is a conglomeration of the most notable and interesting projects that I have worked on, ranging from realtime video processing, and multi-megahertz embedded signal synthesis to a tutorial on porting a videogame from Unity to a Cisco PBX (Technically an ISR but who's counting?).
